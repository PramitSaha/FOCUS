Motivation

How can we efficiently and fairly fine-tune massive foundation models for real-world FL in domains with strict privacy and limited resources?

ðŸ”’ Challenges

(1) Resource Constraints: Fine-tuning foundation models exceeds client resources.
(2) Layer Selection: Naive selection overfits and ignores client-specific needs.
(3) Competing Goals: Balancing client adaptation and collaborative layer usage is hard.

ðŸ”‘ Solutions

Client Layer Importance: NTK spectral analysis scores each layer per client.
Server-Side Layer Balancing: Meta-heuristics maximize adaptation and distribute layer updates.
Privacy-Preserving: Optimization happens without sharing raw data.

Distinction from prior works

(a) Vanilla: selects layers only on local client data, neglecting collaboration.
(b) F3OCUS: jointly maximizes client-specific layer importance and distributes layer adaptation across clients.

Method

Two-step "Define and Refine" strategy:
1. Client: Layer importance scores from NTK principal eigenvalue.
2. Server: Refines layer selection using meta-heuristics (Genetic Algorithms, Bee/Ant Colony, Swarm, Annealing) to maximize adaptation and balance layer usage.
No server data needed.

MedVQA-FL Dataset

MedVQA-FL is a new federated VQA dataset with 707,962 triplets across 12 anatomical categories (Colon, Lung, Skin, Eye, Breast, Kidney, Blood, Femur, Heart, Liver, Pancreas, Spleen), partitioned to simulate 9 modality-specific clients.
[Code & Data Release]

Experiments & Results

- Compared against standard layer selection, pruning, and PEFT baselines.
- Validated on 6 FL tasks: VQA, Image/Text-based Disease Detection, and Heterogeneous settings.
- F3OCUS shows consistent gains in accuracy, fairness, and efficiency.
- Ablation: Meta-heuristics outperform standard optimization, balancing adaptation and collaboration.
