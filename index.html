Motivation

Inspired by the challenge of efficient personalization in federated learning, we believe that leveraging both client-specific needs and layer utilization balance is crucial for scalable and privacy-preserving fine-tuning of foundation models.

ðŸ”’ Challenges

(1) Resource Constraints: Massive Foundation Models require extensive compute and data unfeasible for individual, resource-constrained clients (e.g., hospitals).
(2) Layer Selection Dilemma: Uniform Layer Selection or naive strategies often lead to overfitting, poor generalization, and underutilized client diversity.
(3) Conflicting Objectives: Maximizing local adaptation vs. ensuring overall layer diversity is a multi-objective problem that traditional optimization struggles to solve, especially without access to server data.

ðŸ”‘ Solutions

(1) Client-aware Layer Selection: Use Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) spectral analysis to identify the most impactful layers per client.
(2) Meta-heuristic Server Optimization: Jointly optimize for high-importance and diverse layer participation without server data. Privacy-preserving Scalability: No raw data leaves the client; the approach scales efficiently and respects privacy. (1) Using Latent-level Diffusion model (Stable Diffusion, SD).
